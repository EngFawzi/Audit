Purchase transactions are one of the most sensitive areas in a corporate tax audit because they directly affect deductible expenses, taxable income, and the reliability of financial records. A tax auditor needs to ask whether the purchase is genuine, properly authorised, supported by evidence, correctly classified, and incurred for business purposes (Kishore & Kaavya, 2026).

Section 2.0

Overview of Purchase Cycle

The purchase cycle forms the audit trail examined during tax verification — from initial need identification to payment processing.

The purchase cycle begins when a department identifies a need for goods or services. Each stage creates an audit trail that becomes the main basis for tax verification (Kishore & Kaavya, 2026).

B

Three-Way Matching Process

In a well-controlled system, the purchase order should agree with the goods received note, supplier invoice, payment voucher, and accounting entry — known as the three-way matching process.

📄

Purchase Order

Confirms order details, quantity, price, and supplier information

📦

Goods Received Note

Confirms actual receipt of goods — does quantity match what was ordered?

🧾

Supplier Invoice

Confirms supplier billing — must align with PO and GRN exactly

Mismatch Alert: If the company ordered 100 units, received 100 units, but was invoiced for 150 units — the extra 50 units may indicate overstatement, error, or a fictitious purchase that must be investigated.

Key Audit Insight

The purchase accounting system at NLC India Limited includes purchase requisition, vendor selection, invoice verification, ledger posting, and payment processing — described as essential for financial accuracy, statutory compliance, and effective financial decision-making (Kishore & Kaavya, 2026). The same controls that protect financial accuracy also protect the tax base.

C

Hidden Tax Risks in a Complete Cycle

A purchase cycle may appear complete while still hiding tax risks. Examples include:

💰

Inflated Related-Party Pricing

Genuine payment made to a related supplier at above-market price, reducing taxable income

🏗️

Capital Items as Expenses

Capital assets (machinery, equipment) wrongly posted as repairs or maintenance expenses

🏠

Private Costs as Business

Director personal costs recorded as office supplies or business expenses

Audit Principle: The purchase cycle gives the auditor a starting point — but not the final answer. Every transaction must be tested for commercial substance.

Section 3.0

Key Stages of Purchase Transactions

Each stage in the purchase process carries specific audit significance and potential tax risk exposure.

3.1

Purchase Requisition & Approval

The purchase requisition shows why the purchase is needed and who requested it. From a tax audit perspective, it helps prove business purpose. Approval by an authorised manager strengthens audit reliability and internal control (Kishore & Kaavya, 2026).

✅

Strong Requisition Indicators

  • Identifies requesting department
  • Links to business operations
  • Authorised by proper manager
  • Describes business purpose clearly

Audit Risk: Purchases without business justification or proper authorisation may be challenged during tax audit. The auditor should be able to link the purchase to staff, departments, or business operations.

3.2

Supplier Selection & Purchase Order

Supplier selection helps the auditor assess whether the purchase was commercially reasonable. A normal supplier should have a business profile consistent with the goods or services provided.

Red FlagAudit ConcernRisk Level
Supplier unrelated to transaction typePossible non-business expenseHigh
Missing or incomplete supplier detailsWeak audit trail — cannot verifyHigh
Excessive pricing vs market rateInflated purchase riskMedium
Unusual supplier business typePossible fictitious transactionHigh
3.3

Goods Received, Invoice Verification & Payment

The goods received note confirms whether the company actually received the goods. Where ERP systems are used, they improve efficiency, reduce errors, and increase transparency in financial operations (Kishore & Kaavya, 2026).

DocumentAudit PurposeWhat Auditor Checks
Goods Received NoteConfirms receipt of goods/servicesQuantity, description, date received
Supplier InvoiceConfirms supplier billingAmount, VAT, supplier details, match to GRN
Bank Payment RecordConfirms settlement was madeAccount belongs to correct supplier, no round-tripping

Critical Point: Payment alone does not automatically prove deductibility. The auditor must still check whether payment went to the correct supplier, whether the bank account belongs to the supplier, and whether there is any round-tripping to directors or related parties.

Overall Stage Insight

Internal control literature emphasises authorisation procedures, audit checks, and proper documentation as safeguards against errors and fraud in purchase accounting (Kishore & Kaavya, 2026). Each stage must be examined — not just the final payment.

Section 4.0

Overstatement of Purchases

Purchase overstatement directly reduces taxable income — making it one of the highest-risk areas in corporate tax audits.

A

What Is Purchase Overstatement?

Overstatement occurs when purchase expenses are recorded higher than the actual goods or services acquired. In tax terms, overstatement is serious because it reduces accounting profit and lowers taxable income.

🧾

Fictitious Invoices

Invoices created for goods or services never actually received

🔁

Duplicate Payments

Same invoice processed and paid more than once

📊

Inflated Quantities

Invoice shows more units than were actually received

💸

Excessive Pricing

Prices charged significantly above market or arm's length rates

❌

Unsupported Claims

No documentary evidence to support the recorded purchase

🏗️

Capital Items as Expenses

Long-term assets wrongly recorded as current expenses

B

Tax Exposure Example — RM30,000 Overstatement

Key Warning: Purchase overstatement is not merely a bookkeeping issue — it directly affects tax payable. An unsupported purchase of RM30,000 creates RM10,440 total tax exposure including penalty.

C

Analytical Review Indicators

Overstatement may also be detected through pattern analysis. Problems occur when goods or services supplied do not match the description, agreement, or representation made in the transaction (Siregar & Harahap, 2025).

Analytical IndicatorPossible InterpretationAction Required
Purchases increase sharply (e.g. 40%)Possible overstatement or inflated purchasesDetailed documentary testing
Sales remain stable (+5%)Commercial inconsistency — purchases should track salesInvestigate stockpiling, inflation, or fraud
Gross profit margin decreasesPossible inflated purchases or unrecorded salesCombine analytical with supplier verification
Inventory levels do not increaseGoods paid for but not receivedPhysical inspection required
Audit Strategy

The stronger approach is to combine document testing with analytical review. Modern tax administration increasingly uses audits, electronic checks, validations, and matching of taxpayer information to test the accuracy and completeness of reported information (OECD, 2024).

Section 5.0

Audit Techniques & Examination Procedures

Five key techniques form the foundation of a rigorous purchase transaction tax audit.

5.1

Documentary Testing

Documentary testing is the foundation of purchase audit. For related party or controlled transactions, documentary testing becomes more detailed. The IRBM Transfer Pricing Tax Audit Framework provides that audit examination may include business records, agreements, contracts, transfer pricing documentation, invoices, vouchers, receipts, and electronic records (IRBM, 2025).

Documents to Examine

  • Purchase requisitions & approvals
  • Purchase orders with full details
  • Goods received notes
  • Supplier invoices & contracts
  • Payment vouchers & bank statements
  • Ledger postings & accounting entries

Audit Objective

Confirm that the purchase was:

  • Properly authorised
  • Actually received
  • Correctly recorded
  • Paid to the correct party

⚠ Limitations

  • Documents may be fabricated
  • Collusion between parties may occur
  • Weak records reduce reliability
  • Stamp/signature can be forged

✓ Strengths

  • Creates verifiable audit trail
  • Supports transaction evidence
  • Required by IRBM Framework
  • Foundation for further testing
Key Audit Insight

Documentary evidence is important, but auditors must still evaluate commercial substance. A genuine-looking document does not automatically prove a genuine transaction.

5.2

Analytical Review

Analytical review focuses on patterns rather than individual documents. Modern tax administration increasingly uses audits, electronic checks, validations, and matching of taxpayer information to test accuracy and completeness (OECD, 2024).

What Auditors Compare

  • Purchase trends vs sales volumes
  • Production volume vs raw material usage
  • Inventory movement vs recorded purchases
  • Gross profit margin year-on-year
  • Industry norms vs company ratios

📊 Example Scenario

Purchases increased by 40% while sales increased by only 5%.


Possible risks: inflated purchases, fictitious invoices, inventory irregularities, or profit manipulation — all require investigation.

IndicatorAudit InterpretationNext Step
Purchases ↑ sharplyPossible overstatementTest invoices & supplier
Sales stable or decliningCommercial inconsistencyCross-check inventory
Gross profit ↓Possible inflated purchasesDetailed transaction testing
Key Audit Insight

Analytical review helps identify inconsistencies that documentary testing alone may miss. It supports a risk-based purchase audit rather than a purely manual checking approach (OECD, 2024).

5.3

Supplier Verification & Third-Party Confirmation

Supplier verification confirms whether the supplier exists, operates in the relevant business, and actually supplied the goods or services. BEPS 13 research shows that stricter transfer pricing documentation gives tax authorities more information to conduct targeted audits, especially for related party transactions (Roggeman et al., 2025).

Supplier Verification Checklist

  • Supplier registration verified with authorities
  • Business address confirmed and legitimate
  • Business activity matches transaction type
  • Bank account belongs to correct supplier
  • Ownership links and related-party relationships examined
  • Transaction history reviewed for patterns

🚩 Shell Supplier Indicators

  • No physical address or registration
  • Bank account belongs to unrelated party
  • Supplier provides goods inconsistent with business
  • Multiple companies share same bank account
  • No verifiable transaction history
Key Audit Insight

Supplier verification helps identify shell suppliers and non-arm's length transactions. Where suppliers are related parties, the auditor should test whether prices are arm's length.

5.4a

Physical Inspection

Physical inspection confirms whether purchased assets exist and are used in the business. This is especially important for machinery, vehicles, computers, and renovation costs.

🏗️

Machinery & Equipment

Verify asset physically exists, is operational, and is used for business purposes

🚗

Vehicles

Check registration, business use percentage, and whether private use was excluded

💻

IT & Renovation

Confirm computers, servers, and renovation work are in place and business-related

Audit Note: Physical inspection is not limited to tangible goods. Services should also be verified through output documentation, correspondence, reports, and evidence of service delivery.

5.4b

Classification Review — Capital vs Revenue

Classification review determines whether items should be treated as capital expenditure (asset) or revenue expenditure (deductible expense). Misclassification is a common and significant tax audit issue.

❌ Wrong Treatment

  • Equipment recorded as repairs
  • Machinery claimed as maintenance
  • Renovation as office expenses
  • Vehicles as transport expense

✓ Correct Treatment

  • Equipment → Capital asset
  • Machinery → Capital expenditure
  • Renovation → Capitalise & depreciate
  • Vehicles → Register as fixed asset
📊 Tax Calculation — RM50,000 Misclassification
ItemAmount (RM)
Equipment Wrongly Recorded as Repairs50,000
Add-back to Taxable Income50,000
Additional Tax at 24%12,000
Penalty at 45%5,400
Total Tax Exposure17,400
Key Audit Insight

Classification errors may significantly understate taxable income. A RM50,000 misclassification produces RM17,400 total tax exposure. The auditor should not rely only on the account title — the actual nature of the purchase must be examined.

Section 6.0

Related Issues in Purchase Transactions

Three critical issues create elevated tax audit risk in purchase transactions: related-party pricing, capital/revenue misclassification, and private expenses.

6.1

Non-Arm's Length & Related Party Purchases

Related party purchases raise significant tax audit risk because the supplier and purchaser may not negotiate independently. The IRBM Transfer Pricing Tax Audit Framework provides that transfer pricing reviews look at business records and financial arrangements to ensure controlled transactions comply with the arm's length principle (IRBM, 2025).

Who Qualifies as Related Party?

  • Subsidiaries or parent companies
  • Director-controlled suppliers
  • Family-linked businesses
  • Companies with common shareholders
  • Entities under common management

⚠ How Profit Shifting Works

Company buys goods from related supplier at RM150/unit instead of market price of RM100/unit. The extra RM50/unit reduces taxable income without commercial justification.

Independent Supplier

Regulatory Reference

The IRBM Framework allows audit officers to examine agreements, contracts, transfer pricing documents, invoices, vouchers, electronic records, and supporting materials to confirm reported income and tax payable (IRBM, 2025).

6.2

Capital Versus Revenue Expenditure Errors

Misclassification occurs when an asset with long-term business benefit is recorded as ordinary expenses. Proper documentation, internal controls, ERP usage, and accurate transaction recording improve financial efficiency (Kishore & Kaavya, 2026). The auditor should not rely only on the account title but should examine the actual nature of the purchase.

Capital Expenditure (Must be Capitalised)

  • Machinery & heavy equipment
  • Renovation & structural works
  • Computer hardware & servers
  • Motor vehicles

Revenue Expenditure (Deductible)

  • Routine repairs & maintenance
  • Day-to-day supplies
  • Software subscriptions (short-term)
  • Office consumables

6.3

Disallowed & Private Expenses

Private or non-business expenses may be incorrectly recorded as business purchases. Transaction descriptions should mirror the real substance of what was supplied — when ledger says "business expense" but actual benefit belongs to the owner or director, this becomes a tax audit concern (Siregar & Harahap, 2025). OECD (2024) notes that tax administrations increasingly use audits, electronic checks, and matching of taxpayer information to test completeness and accuracy.

Expense TypeRecorded AsActual NatureAudit Concern

Family mealsEntertainment expenseNon-business purposeNon-deductible
Personal travelBusiness travelPrivate holidayNon-deductible
Luxury purchasesOffice suppliesPersonal benefitQuestionable

Substance Over Form: A genuine invoice does not automatically prove business deductibility. The auditor must assess whether the expense was truly incurred for business purposes, regardless of how it is labelled in the ledger.

Risk Indicators

Key Audit Red Flags

These indicators, when identified during audit, require immediate deeper investigation and may signal tax understatement.

Red flags do not automatically prove fraud or tax evasion — but they indicate areas requiring heightened audit scrutiny. Modern tax administration increasingly uses data matching and analytical tools to identify these patterns (OECD, 2024).

📋

Duplicate Invoice Numbers

Same invoice number appears more than once — risk of fictitious or duplicate purchases being claimed twice

📅

Large Year-End Purchases

Significant spike in purchases near year-end with no operational justification — classic profit manipulation technique

💰

Excessive Related-Party Pricing

Prices significantly above market rates from connected suppliers — indicates transfer pricing risk and profit shifting

🏭

Missing Supplier Information

No physical address, registration number, or verifiable business activity — weak audit trail, possible shell entity

🏠

Private Expenses in Purchase Ledger

Personal or non-business expenses recorded as deductible purchases — reduces taxable income improperly

🔧

Capital Items as Repairs

Machinery, equipment, or renovation recorded as maintenance expense — immediate full deduction instead of depreciation

🔄

Round-Amount Invoices

Invoices consistently showing round figures (RM10,000, RM50,000) without decimal breakdown — unusual in genuine commercial transactions

🏦

Multiple Suppliers, Same Bank Account

Different supplier names but payments go to identical bank account — suggests single entity controlling multiple fictitious suppliers

📊

Purchases Rise, Sales Stable

Purchases increase significantly while sales remain flat — analytically inconsistent, requires detailed investigation

Section 7.0

Conclusion

Purchase transaction audit is both a documentation review and a substance-based tax examination.

Purchase transaction audit should be approached as both a documentation review and a substance-based tax examination. A complete invoice, purchase order, and payment voucher may support the transaction, but they do not automatically prove that the deduction is valid.

✓

Key Findings

1. Audit Trail Starts with the Cycle

The purchase cycle provides the audit trail — from requisition to payment. While ERP systems improve efficiency, they do not eliminate audit risk (Kishore & Kaavya, 2026).

2. Documentation Is Necessary but Insufficient

Documentary testing confirms basic evidence. But fabricated documents, collusion, and classification errors can still occur even when paperwork looks complete.

3. Analytical Review Detects Hidden Risks

Pattern analysis — comparing purchases, sales, inventory, and margins — identifies inconsistencies that documentary testing alone may miss (OECD, 2024).

4. Substance Over Form Always Prevails

Whether an expense is deductible depends on whether it was genuinely incurred for business purposes — not whether it has a supporting invoice (Siregar & Harahap, 2025).

Final Takeaway

Purchase audit is not only about checking what the company paid. It is about understanding what the company actually received, who supplied it, how it was priced, how it was recorded, and whether the business genuinely benefited. That is where purchase audit becomes a meaningful tax audit technique.

Practical Impact: The calculations demonstrate that unsupported purchases of RM30,000 create RM10,440 total exposure, while misclassification of RM50,000 creates RM17,400 — a combined total of RM27,840. Strong internal controls and accurate classification prevent these outcomes entirely.
